history

BSC Young Boys, commonly known as Young Boys, is a Swiss football club based in Bern. The club was founded in 1898 by four students—Max Schwab, Hermann Bauer, Franz Kehrli, and Oskar Schwab. The name Young Boys was inspired by the English club Old Boys FC. Young Boys won their first Swiss championship in 1903 and quickly became one of the country’s dominant teams. They secured multiple league titles in the early 1900s, establishing themselves as a force in Swiss football.

After World War II, Young Boys struggled but regained their strength in the 1950s, winning four consecutive Swiss championships from 1957 to 1960. This era was marked by the leadership of coach Albert Sing and star players like Eugen Meier.

Under Gerardo Seoane, Young Boys won the Swiss Super League in 2018, ending a 32-year title drought.
